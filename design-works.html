Design Works

Designs born from the question:
What if V = N / D could be given physical form?
Every object here was designed before the formula had a name. A pendant that asks yes or no. A clock that measures sound instead of time. An engine that rejects reaction force. A proposal to stop an oil spill with geometry.

These are not products. They are hypotheses — made tangible.
Concept / System Design

慣性制御エンジン
Inertia Control Engine

A propulsion concept designed before the GKU theory had words. The intuition: movement without reaction. N without D. Later formalized as the Non-Reaction Propulsion Theory (PRT).

Concept / System Design

姿勢制御
Attitude Control

How does a body — object or human — maintain orientation in an environment with no fixed reference point? A question that became a philosophy.

Concept / System Design

Rocket-c

A propulsion form study. Geometry as argument. The shape itself is the hypothesis.

Product Design

yes or no pendant

A wearable decision instrument. Not jewelry — a philosophical tool. The design asks: can a single object reduce the friction of choice? V = N / D applied to the most human of problems: deciding.

Product Design

Sound clock

Time measured not by rotation, but by resonance. A clock that tells you where you are in sound, not in hours. The first object in this series to use acoustic space as a design material.

Product Design

time pressure

Pressure visualized as time. Or time visualized as pressure. A study in the relationship between force and duration — two variables that V = N / D would later formalize as D.

Product Design

MARK

To mark is to make permanent. Koku (刻). This object is about the act of inscription — leaving a trace that outlasts the hand that made it.

Space / Philosophy

tool for hear sound out of cosmos

What would a device look like that listens to the universe? Not a radio telescope. A personal instrument. Designed for the individual who wants to know if anyone is out there.

Space / Philosophy

Out of Cosmos sound

If the cosmos makes sound, what does it sound like? And what kind of object helps a human being receive it?

Space / Philosophy

宇宙一個
One Universe

A single object that contains the idea of a universe. Minimal form. Maximum implication. The design question: what is the smallest thing that holds everything?

Social Design

メキシコ海底油田
Gulf Oil Spill — Solution Proposal

April 2010. The Deepwater Horizon disaster. A geometric containment system based on pressure differential principles. Social problems are design problems. D can always be reduced.

Design Theory

アフォーダンス
Affordance

A direct engagement with James Gibson's affordance theory. What does an object invite you to do? And what happens when the invitation is designed to reduce friction?

Process

idea sketch

The raw material of design. Before every object, there is a line on paper. Included here because the process is part of the work.

Observation

yashaga

A place. A moment. A record. Not every design begins with a problem. Some begin with the act of seeing.

Observation / Photography

Night cafe "SIX" in Fukui City

Fukui, Japan. The city where all of this was designed. A night café. The kind of place where ideas arrive uninvited. This is where V = N / D was born.

Yoshimitsu Katayama is a designer based in Fukui, Japan.
His work spans product design, concept systems, and the intersection
of physical form with philosophical inquiry.

All works shown here predate the V = N / D formula —
and in retrospect, every one of them was trying to find it.
